Smart home automation enables energy savings that would be impractical to achieve manually. By connecting devices to a central hub or energy management system, you can respond automatically to electricity prices, weather conditions, and solar generation levels.

Smart Thermostat Features to Enable:
- Geofencing: thermostat detects when all occupants leave and switches to away mode automatically
- Learning mode: the thermostat builds a schedule from your manual adjustments over 1–2 weeks
- Demand response integration: allows your utility to briefly adjust your setpoint during grid emergencies in exchange for bill credits
- Energy usage reports: weekly emails showing kWh consumed by heating and cooling help identify trends

Automating Appliances Around Electricity Prices:
- Connect smart plugs to dishwashers, washing machines, and dryers and create rules that delay their start until off-peak hours
- Smart outlets can monitor standby power draw and cut power to devices on a schedule
- Pair smart plugs with an energy monitoring app to see real-time cost per device
- Set price triggers: if the current electricity rate is above a threshold, pause non-essential loads automatically

Solar-Responsive Automation:
- Configure your home automation hub to fetch solar generation data every 15–30 minutes
- Create rules such as: "when solar generation exceeds 2 kW, start the dishwasher and washing machine"
- Water heaters with smart controls can be set to boost temperature during peak solar generation, storing thermal energy for evening use
- EV chargers with solar-matching mode will ramp charging speed up and down to track available solar output

Lighting Automation:
- Motion sensors in hallways, bathrooms, and garages ensure lights are never left on accidentally
- Occupancy-based lighting in rooms with irregular use (home offices, guest rooms) typically saves 30–45% on lighting energy
- Schedule outdoor lights to turn off after midnight and use dimming rather than full brightness where security permits
- Daylight harvesting: smart switches automatically dim artificial lights when sufficient natural light is available

Smart Power Strips and Vampire Load Elimination:
- Vampire (standby) load accounts for 5–10% of the average household's electricity use
- Use smart power strips in home theatre setups: when the TV turns off, all connected devices lose power
- Set a daily schedule on smart plugs for phone chargers, printers, and gaming consoles to cut power during sleeping hours
- Some smart plugs report energy use by the hour, making it easy to identify the worst offenders

Voice and App Control Best Practices:
- Group devices by room so one command adjusts lighting, temperature, and plugs together
- Create an "all off" routine triggered when you say goodnight or tap a single button by the front door
- Review the automation log weekly to confirm rules are firing as intended and catching savings
- Integrate your energy management system with weather forecasts so automations can anticipate cold snaps or heatwaves
